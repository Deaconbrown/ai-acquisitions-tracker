This week's biggest deal
Scottish AI firm with strong links to Belfast acquired by OpenAI as part of $4bn investment
Reported by The Irish News →

OpenAI's acquisition of a Scottish AI firm, framed inside a $4 billion investment package, signals that the company is moving beyond model development and into applied capability across European talent markets. Scotland and Belfast have quietly built strong AI research communities, and this deal suggests OpenAI is buying access to that talent base before rivals can. A $4 billion envelope also implies this is not a single transaction but a broader programme of regional acquisitions and partnerships. For founders in the UK and Ireland, this is a signal that the largest AI labs are now actively shopping outside Silicon Valley.

What to watch
Three patterns stand out this week. First, the largest AI labs are acquiring outside their home markets, with OpenAI moving into Scotland and Belfast, and Numero pulling in talent from Chennai. Geography is no longer a filter. Second, vertical software is becoming an acquisition target again, with deals in legal research, retail marketing, and finance and accounting all closing in the same week. AI companies are buying domain depth rather than building it. Third, the hardware layer is tightening. Credo's purchase of DustPhotonics points to a race to control the physical interconnect infrastructure that moves data between chips at the speeds AI workloads demand. Watch for more photonics and networking acquisitions as hyperscalers and chip firms compete to remove the bottleneck between compute and memory.
