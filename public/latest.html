This week's biggest deal
China Blocks Meta's Acquisition of AI Startup Manus, Weeks Before Trump-Xi Summit
Reported by Bloomberg →

Beijing's decision to block Meta's acquisition of Manus is the clearest signal yet that AI dealmaking is now a direct instrument of geopolitical leverage, not just commercial strategy. China used the Manus deal as a chip ahead of the Trump-Xi summit, demonstrating it can veto Western tech acquisitions involving Chinese-linked assets without firing a shot. For founders and investors, this changes the risk calculus on any AI startup with Chinese roots, Chinese capital, or Chinese engineering talent, regardless of where it is incorporated. Expect acquirers to add geopolitical due diligence as a standard deal condition, and expect more deals to quietly collapse before they ever reach the press.

What to watch
Two themes dominate this week. First, AI is moving deep into vertical industries. Procore buying Datagrid, IKS Health acquiring ARAI, HBX Group taking Bridgify, and UFOFLEET absorbing FleetMaster all point to the same conviction: generic AI is a commodity, and the value now lives in domain-specific models and workflows embedded inside existing enterprise software. Second, and more consequentially, geopolitics is becoming the deciding variable in cross-border AI deals. Beijing's block of Meta's Manus acquisition will not be the last intervention of this kind. Watch for acquirers to restructure deal terms to ring-fence assets from regulatory risk, for founders with any China exposure to face harder questions in due diligence, and for governments beyond China to consider whether they want similar levers over AI talent and IP flowing across their borders.
