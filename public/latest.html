This week's biggest deal
Cohere Acquires Germany's Aleph Alpha in Sovereign AI Push
Reported by observer.com →

Cohere's acquisition of Aleph Alpha is the clearest signal yet that the sovereign AI market has moved from political talking point to commercial reality. Aleph Alpha built its reputation serving European governments and enterprises that refused to route sensitive workloads through US hyperscalers, giving Cohere an immediate foothold in a segment where trust and data residency matter more than raw benchmark performance. For Aidan Gomez, the deal trades Cohere's pure model business for something harder to replicate: deep government relationships and the regulatory credibility that comes with them. It also positions Cohere as the default enterprise AI layer for European institutions at exactly the moment the EU is accelerating digital sovereignty spending.

What to watch
This week's deals point to three converging pressures. First, sovereign AI is now a viable commercial category, not just a policy aspiration. Cohere's Aleph Alpha move will push other foundation model companies to seek similar footholds in Europe, the Middle East, and Asia before those markets consolidate. Second, the Meta and Manus situation shows that cross-border AI talent acquisition is entering genuinely contested territory. Beijing's decision to block the Manus founders from leaving signals that governments are prepared to treat AI researchers as strategic assets. Third, the physical layer of AI is attracting serious capital, with Vertiv buying BMarko and Skild absorbing Zebra's robotics unit in the same week. Watch for more infrastructure and hardware deals as the gap between AI model capability and real-world deployment capacity forces acquirers downstream into manufacturing and automation.
