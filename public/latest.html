This week's biggest deal
Anthropic Acquires Startup Coefficient Bio for About $400 Million
Reported by The Information →

Anthropic paying roughly $400 million for Coefficient Bio is the clearest sign yet that frontier AI labs view biology as their next major battleground, not a distant adjacency. The deal is large enough to signal conviction, not experimentation. For Anthropic, adding wet-lab computational biology capability accelerates a push into scientific research applications where Claude already has strong enterprise traction. Rivals at OpenAI and Google DeepMind have made similar moves, so any lab that sits out the life sciences race risks ceding a market that could dwarf consumer AI in long-run value.

What to watch
Three patterns stand out this week. First, frontier labs are moving beyond software. Anthropic buying Coefficient Bio and Meta acquiring the Limitless pendant show that the leading AI companies now see physical and biological systems as core product territory, not future optionality. Second, infrastructure control is tightening. Nvidia absorbing SchedMD, which stewards the Slurm workload scheduler that runs on a large share of the world's HPC clusters, puts the chip giant closer to the software layer that allocates its own hardware. That vertical grip will draw regulatory attention. Third, European AI capacity is consolidating into US and US-backed hands, with Cohere taking Aleph Alpha. Watch for more cross-border deals as European governments weigh whether to intervene, and watch Anthropic's next move: a $400 million life sciences bet implies further acquisitions to build out the research pipeline around it.
