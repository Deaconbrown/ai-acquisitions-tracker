This week's biggest deal
Workday Acquires AI Startup Sana For $1.1 Billion
Reported by Yahoo Finance →

Workday's $1.1 billion purchase of Sana is the largest disclosed deal of the week and marks a decisive move by the HR and finance software giant to embed AI learning and knowledge management directly into its platform. At that price, Workday is paying a premium for speed, buying a capability it could not build fast enough on its own as rivals including SAP and Oracle push comparable AI features into enterprise workflows. The deal signals that established SaaS vendors are now willing to write nine and ten figure cheques to avoid being left behind in the agent era, where the quality of an AI layer increasingly determines platform stickiness. Founders building vertical AI tools inside large software categories should read this as validation that strategic acquirers are back at the table with serious capital.

What to watch
This week's deal flow reveals three distinct races running in parallel. Meta is buying its way into physical AI, acquiring both a robotics company and Limitless, an AI wearable backed by Sam Altman, signalling that the next platform war will be fought on the body and in the home, not just on the screen. OpenAI is moving in two directions at once, adding voice cloning through Weights.gg while pushing into fintech with Roi, which suggests the company is building toward a broader consumer and financial services layer rather than staying narrowly focused on model capability. Meanwhile, enterprise software incumbents including Workday and Zendesk are buying agent and workflow AI to defend their positions against leaner competitors. Watch for more nine-figure acquisitions from SaaS vendors in the HR, finance, and customer service categories, and watch whether Meta's hardware buying spree accelerates pressure on Apple and Google to respond with their own wearable AI deals.
