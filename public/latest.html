This week's biggest deal
SpaceX Set Up a $60 Billion Deal to Buy xAI
Reported by The Motley Fool →

A $60 billion deal between SpaceX and xAI would be the largest AI acquisition ever attempted, and its structure matters as much as its size. By routing the deal through SpaceX rather than a public vehicle, Elon Musk keeps regulatory scrutiny at a distance while binding his rocket and satellite infrastructure directly to his AI ambitions. xAI gains access to SpaceX's compute, data, and global connectivity assets without a separate fundraise or IPO. For investors watching AI consolidation, this signals that the next phase of the arms race is vertical, folding AI labs into hardware and infrastructure empires rather than leaving them as standalone software bets.

What to watch
This week's deals reveal two separate consolidation currents running at once. At the top of the market, the largest players are buying physical and social infrastructure, with Meta picking up both a humanoid robotics firm and an AI social network in the same week, and the SpaceX deal signalling that AI labs will increasingly be absorbed into asset-heavy conglomerates rather than standing alone. Below that, mid-market deals in risk, governance, cargo inspection, and enterprise connectivity point to a quieter but durable wave: buyers are plugging AI capabilities into regulated, high-stakes workflows where explainability and audit trails matter. Watch for more governance-focused acquisitions as regulators in the US and EU tighten AI oversight requirements. The SpaceX and xAI situation will also set a precedent for how pre-IPO structures can be used to absorb large AI assets without triggering the public-market scrutiny that a straightforward acquisition would invite.
