This week's biggest deal
SpaceX strikes $60 billion deal for the right to buy AI coding startup Cursor
Reported by Yahoo Finance →

A $60 billion option on an AI coding tool is not a conventional acquisition. It is a statement about where Elon Musk believes software development is heading and who he wants controlling the picks and shovels. Cursor, built by Anysphere, has become the dominant AI coding assistant for professional developers, which makes it a strategic chokepoint in the software supply chain. SpaceX securing the right to buy rather than buying outright suggests the deal is as much about blocking rivals as it is about immediate integration.

What to watch
This week's deals cluster around two themes: control of critical infrastructure and the expansion of AI into regulated, high-stakes sectors. SpaceX optioning Cursor, MARA buying Long Ridge, and Scale AI absorbing ICG all point to the same logic. The companies that win the next phase of AI competition will be those that own the compute, the tooling, and the government access simultaneously. The Meta robotics move adds a physical world dimension to that race. Watch for more option-style deals in the weeks ahead, where acquirers want leverage without full commitment, and watch for defence and intelligence contractors to become premium targets as national security spending on AI accelerates.
