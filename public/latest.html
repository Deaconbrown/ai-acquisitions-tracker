This week's biggest deal
Beijing blocks Meta's acquisition of AI start-up Manus, amid US-China rivalry
Reported by Taipei Times →

Beijing's decision to block Meta's bid for Manus marks a significant escalation in the use of regulatory power as a weapon in the US-China technology rivalry. China is signalling that its most promising AI assets are not for sale to American buyers, regardless of price. For Meta, the blocked deal exposes the limits of its AI acquisition strategy in a world where geopolitics can veto commercial logic. Founders and investors backing Chinese AI startups must now factor in a new constraint: a Beijing veto may cap their exit options to Western acquirers.

What to watch
Three patterns define this week. First, geopolitics is becoming a hard constraint on deal flow. Beijing's veto of the Manus deal will not be the last, and acquirers targeting Chinese AI assets now need a regulatory strategy as much as a commercial one. Second, large incumbents are buying their way into new interface layers. Atlassian's $610 million bet on Dia and Apple's headline acquisition both suggest the next battleground is not models but the surfaces through which users reach them. Third, vertical AI consolidation is accelerating quietly in healthcare and dental imaging, where Azra AI, Thynk, Overjet, and DentalBee are staking out defensible niches before a larger wave of rollups arrives. Watch for more blocked cross-border deals in the coming weeks, and watch whether SAP's inability to convert acquisitions into share price gains prompts other enterprise software buyers to pause and reassess their own spending pace.
