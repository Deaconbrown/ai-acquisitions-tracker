Meta unwinds $2 billion Manus acquisition after China order
Reported by qz.com →

Meta's decision to unwind the $2 billion acquisition of Manus, a virtual reality company, due to a Chinese government order highlights the geopolitical risks now embedded in high-value AI and deep tech deals. This move underscores how global regulatory environments and national security concerns can override even significant strategic investments. For acquirers, it's a stark reminder that due diligence must now encompass a thorough understanding of international regulatory landscapes.

What to watch
This week saw a flurry of acquisitions targeting AI capabilities across various sectors, from cybersecurity and marketing to real estate and aerospace. Notably, companies are focusing on integrating AI for enhanced decision-making, specialized applications, and operational efficiency. The trend indicates a clear strategy among established firms to bolster their AI portfolios rather than solely relying on organic development. We will continue to watch for consolidations that aim to capture niche AI expertise and talent, particularly in areas like sovereign AI and industry-specific solutions.
