This week's biggest deal
SpaceX acquires xAI in bid to develop orbital data centers
Reported by SpaceNews →

SpaceX absorbing xAI is the most structurally consequential deal of the week, folding Elon Musk's AI lab directly into his rocket company and pointing toward a future where compute infrastructure orbits the Earth rather than sits in a terrestrial data center. The strategic logic is blunt: launch costs are falling, spectrum is plentiful in low Earth orbit, and collocating inference hardware with Starlink satellites could give xAI latency and energy advantages that no ground-based rival can easily replicate. For investors, this is a reminder that the AI infrastructure race is no longer confined to land, and that vertical integration now extends into space. Any competitor without a credible launch partner is, by definition, one constraint away from being outpaced.

What to watch
Three distinct forces ran through this week's deals. First, geopolitics is now an active deal-killer: Beijing's block on Meta's Manus acquisition confirms that AI assets are treated as strategic goods, not commercial ones, and any cross-border deal involving Chinese-founded teams will face state-level scrutiny. Second, sovereign AI is real money: Cohere buying Aleph Alpha plants a Canadian AI champion inside Europe's most AI-cautious regulatory environment, a bet that governments will pay a premium for models they can call their own. Third, infrastructure is escaping gravity: the SpaceX and xAI merger points toward orbital compute as the next frontier in the data center arms race. Watch for more vertical integration between launch providers and AI labs, more government-backed consolidation in Europe, and more regulatory blocking of acquisitions wherever China perceives strategic loss.
