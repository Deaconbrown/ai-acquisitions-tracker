This week's biggest deal
SpaceX strikes $60 billion deal for the right to buy AI coding startup Cursor
Reported by Yahoo Finance →

This reported $60 billion deal for the right to acquire Cursor by SpaceX highlights a significant trend. Large corporations are increasingly looking to secure strategic AI capabilities, even at substantial valuations. The move suggests a desire to integrate advanced AI, particularly in coding, directly into core operations, signaling a focus on internal AI development rather than relying on external tools. This acquisition by a space exploration and technology giant underscores the broad applicability and strategic importance of AI across diverse industries.

What to watch
This week's acquisitions demonstrate a clear pattern: established companies are aggressively acquiring AI capabilities to enhance their core businesses, from real estate to travel to software development. The sheer volume of deals, particularly those involving specialized AI tools, suggests a market maturing with consolidation. Expect continued strategic acquisitions as companies race to embed AI across all functions and build proprietary AI advantages, focusing on niche solutions that offer immediate operational or strategic benefits.
