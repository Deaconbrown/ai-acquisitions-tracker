This week's biggest deal
Cohere Acquires Germany's Aleph Alpha in Sovereign AI Push
Reported by observer.com →

Cohere's acquisition of Aleph Alpha is the clearest signal yet that enterprise AI is consolidating around sovereign infrastructure, not just model capability. Aleph Alpha built its reputation serving European governments and regulated industries that will not route sensitive data through US hyperscalers, and Cohere has made the same bet with its own customer base. Aidan Gomez gains a credible European foothold, a portfolio of government contracts, and a team that understands public sector procurement in ways most North American AI companies do not. For investors watching the sovereign AI theme, this deal confirms that regulatory geography is now a genuine competitive moat.

What to watch
Three forces are shaping this week's deal flow. First, geopolitical friction is now a deal variable, not a footnote. Beijing blocking the Meta and Manus deal, and Cohere buying Aleph Alpha for its European government relationships, show that where AI runs is becoming as contested as how well it runs. Second, vertical integration is accelerating. SpaceX absorbing xAI to build orbital compute, and Azra AI buying Thynk to deepen a clinical workflow platform, both reflect the same logic: own the full stack or lose the margin. Third, smaller acqui-hires and sub-$20 million deals continue to multiply quietly, with Capacity, BridgeWise, and Legora all moving to buy specific capabilities rather than build them. Watch for European regulators to respond to the Cohere and Aleph Alpha combination, and watch whether Beijing's blocking of the Manus deal accelerates the formation of parallel Chinese AI acquisition markets closed to Western buyers.
