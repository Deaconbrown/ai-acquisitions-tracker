This week's biggest deal
Meta acquires Moltbook, the Reddit-like network for AI agents
Reported by The Verge →

Meta acquiring Moltbook, a social network built for AI agents rather than humans, signals that the company is not simply building AI tools but is investing in the infrastructure that lets autonomous agents communicate and coordinate at scale. This is a different kind of bet from the typical talent or model acquisition. Meta appears to be positioning itself as the owner of the social layer that AI agents will inhabit, which would give it a structural advantage as agentic AI moves from research into everyday deployment. Combined with its separate robotics push this week, the pattern is clear: Meta wants to own the space where AI agents live, work, and interact.

What to watch
This week's deals point to two converging forces. First, large platforms are buying the social and coordination layers that agents will need, not just the models themselves. Meta's Moltbook deal is the clearest signal yet. Second, geopolitics is now a live variable in AI M&A: the reported collapse of the Manus deal over Chinese regulatory pressure shows that cross-border acquisitions involving Chinese-linked AI firms face a new and unpredictable approval risk. Watch for acquirers to quietly shift their target lists toward firms with clean jurisdictional profiles. In vertical markets, healthcare and enterprise software continue to draw the most activity, with IKS Health, Zendesk, and Mphasis all moving in the same week. Agentic AI infrastructure is the theme buyers are paying up for right now.
