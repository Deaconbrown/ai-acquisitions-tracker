This week's biggest deal
Apple buys secretive Israeli AI company Q.ai for $1.6 billion in biggest acquisition of its history
Reported by The Jerusalem Post →

A $1.6 billion price tag for a company most people have never heard of tells you everything about how Apple views the AI race. Q.ai operated in deliberate secrecy, which suggests Apple was buying deep research capability or proprietary model architecture rather than a consumer brand. At this valuation, the deal dwarfs Apple's typical acqui-hire and signals that Tim Cook's team is willing to spend at a scale previously reserved for platform-defining bets. The Jerusalem Post's reporting on an Israeli AI company landing Apple's largest ever acquisition also underscores how seriously the US-Israel technology corridor has become a hunting ground for frontier AI talent.

What to watch
Three patterns stand out this week. First, physical AI is now a boardroom priority. Meta's robotics acquisition, whatever the exact target, confirms that the major platforms are moving beyond software and into machines that act in the world. Second, geopolitics is shaping deal flow in real time. Meta's reported retreat from the Manus deal after Chinese regulatory pressure is a warning to any acquirer eyeing a company with ties to China. Third, vertical AI is maturing fast. Procore buying Datagrid for construction, Microsoft buying Fintool for finance, and HBX Group buying Bridgify for travel all point to the same conclusion: generic AI tools are losing ground to purpose-built platforms with proprietary data and workflow integration. Watch for more vertical specialists to receive acquisition approaches in the weeks ahead, particularly in regulated industries where compliance requirements give incumbents a structural advantage over new entrants.
