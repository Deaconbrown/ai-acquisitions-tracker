This week's biggest deal
SpaceX Acquires xAI in Bid to Develop Orbital Data Centers
Reported by SpaceNews →

SpaceX absorbing xAI is the most structurally significant deal of the week, collapsing the boundary between Elon Musk's rocket company and his AI lab into a single entity with ambitions that stretch literally off the planet. Orbital data centers would give xAI compute that sits outside any single nation's regulatory reach, a strategic advantage that no terrestrial hyperscaler can easily replicate. The deal also consolidates Musk's two most capital-intensive ventures under unified ownership, reducing friction over shared resources such as power, cooling, and connectivity. If the orbital compute thesis proves viable, this acquisition could redefine where AI infrastructure is built and who controls access to it.

What to watch
Three themes dominate this week. First, robotics is becoming an acquisition category in its own right. Meta buying a robotics AI startup, Skild AI absorbing Zebra Technologies' robotics automation unit, and CP Group taking Recycleye all point to a conviction that physical AI is moving from research to deployment at pace. Second, geopolitics is now a deal-killer. Beijing blocking Meta's bid for Manus is a sharp reminder that Chinese-founded AI assets are subject to export controls that run in both directions, and any buyer targeting a cross-border AI deal should treat regulatory approval as the primary risk, not an afterthought. Third, the OpenAI acquisition of Weights.gg signals that voice and audio AI capabilities are scarce enough to acquire quietly rather than build. Watch for more sub-threshold acqui-hires in audio, vision, and multimodal AI as the frontier labs race to close capability gaps before competitors do.
