This week's biggest deal
SpaceX strikes $60 billion deal for the right to buy AI coding startup Cursor
Reported by Yahoo Finance →

This reported $60 billion deal, while not an acquisition but the right to buy, signifies a major strategic move for SpaceX. Acquiring Cursor, an AI coding assistant, suggests a deep integration of AI into SpaceX's complex engineering and software development processes. The massive valuation indicates a belief in AI's transformative power for productivity, especially in highly technical fields. This move signals a trend of major tech players, even those not traditionally seen as AI-first, investing heavily to control their AI destiny.

What to watch
This week shows a clear consolidation trend in AI tooling and infrastructure, with Canva and Altair making significant strategic acquisitions. Altair, in particular, is on an aggressive acquisition spree, bolstering its capabilities across multiple engineering disciplines. The potential for major players like SpaceX to vertically integrate AI tools is a growing theme. We expect to see continued M&A activity as companies seek to secure essential AI capabilities and talent, potentially leading to further market segmentation and consolidation.
